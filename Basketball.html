About Basketball

Basketball is a popular team sport where two teams of 5 players score by shooting a ball through an elevated hoop (basket) on a rectangular court, using skills like dribbling, passing, and shooting to outscore opponents, a game invented in 1891 by James Naismith. Points are awarded for shots (2 or 3 points), and the game involves offense (scoring) and defense (preventing scores), with rules governing movement and fouls. Inventor: Naismith in 1891 for indoor fitness. Evolution: Started with peach baskets; evolved with metal hoops, backboards, and rules like the three-point shot. Global Sport: Popular worldwide, with major leagues like the NBA (USA) and FIBA (International).
