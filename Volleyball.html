About Volleyball

Volleyball is a popular team sport where two teams of six players hit a ball over a high net, aiming to ground it on the opponent's court, with each team allowed three touches before returning it. Invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan as a less vigorous alternative to basketball, it's played globally, involving skills like serving, passing, setting, and spiking, with points scored on successful plays or opponent errors, played in sets, typically to 25 points.
